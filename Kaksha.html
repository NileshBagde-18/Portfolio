Kaksha - Classroom From Home

Timeline  

# June - July 2021

My Role

#  Solo Project

Project Discription

Children friendly and fun online classroom app for students and teachers.

Focused Area

Better User Experience and simple User Interface for Children and Teacher.

Purpose

Learn with fun
To create a better teaching and learning experience
Tracking performance
Improve attendance
Make tests fun and rewarding

1. Market Overview

1. Children spend their average 6-9 hours in schools.
2. This has decreased into 4-5 hours (average) because of lockdown and online classes.
3. Although the the hours decreased the students are not willing to attend the lectures due to lack of interaction and intrest.
4.Students are sometimes missing tests because of poor notifications and no reminders.
5.childrens are having trouble in understanding due to lack of visuals.

2. Problem

1. No classroom app is friendly to children.
2. They are business meeting app which schools are using and are not a children friendly
3. Test are missed due to attendance.
4. Kids are lacking in studies due to poor interaction experience.

3. Design Solutions

1.The App that allows students to have a friendly interface.
2. Test that are rewarding
3. Playful interface to create more interest.
4.Integrate calendar, reminder, screen widgetsalrm
5.integrate gif and image search.

4.User Research

Target User
Age Group : 3-16
Primary: Students
Secondary: Teachers

A questionnaire was shared among selected list of users and their feedbacks were noted to get the required insights :
1. Do you thinks the online classroom look fun ?
2. Do you attend tests?
3. Do you find yourself engage in one to one interaction?
4. Can you ask for a break using apps?
5. Can you understand Timetable and calendar?
6. How much do you use other interactions provided inn the apps?
7. Are the tests rewarding?
Observation from Answers :
- Most of the childrens are not using timetable as before Because of complex design
- Most of the childrens are becoming lazy , less interactive and they miss tests.
- Tests are not rewarding.
